1st Workshop on
Artificial Intelligence for the Cryosphere (WAIC)

AN INITIATIVE OF THE POLAR AI TASK FORCE

As the Arctic and Antarctic change with a changing climate, AI can be a key tool in research and deployment.

This inaugural workshop is a unique opportunity to gather researchers and practitioners from around the world to streamline emerging work at the intersection of machine learning and the polar sciences.

Thomas Y. Chen

Polar AI Task Force Chair

Welcome to the 1st Workshop on AI for the Cryosphere (WAIC)!

Artificial intelligence (AI) has in recent years begun playing a unique role as a tool for assessing the impacts of climate change in the Arctic and Antarctic, from marine, geophysical, atmospheric, and ecological perspectives. As the polar regions are affected most severely by a warming globe, it is crucial that automated computational systems are in place to monitor biodiversity, track sea ice movement, and analyze glaciers, the Arctic Ocean, and the Southern Ocean. Due to recent interest in the burgeoning intersection between AI and the cryospheric sciences among a diverse set of international scientists and other stakeholders like industry and government, the Polar AI Task Force (aipolar.github.io) was formed. The task force seeks to streamline research at this nexus and fill in the gaps in terms of areas of future work and improvement. This group of researchers, from at least twenty countries, is segmented into a number of topic-specific and geography-specific working groups, including AI for sea ice, AI for Greenland, AI for marine ecology, and AI for the Antarctic. As a group of scientists and industry practitioners, we emphasize the importance of not only a coherent work plan for the development of state-of-the-art models but also successful deployment of algorithms for real-world impacts in the Arctic and Antarctic. At the same time, challenges such as the limited interpretability of algorithms like neural networks and the vast computing resources and energy consumption needed to train complex models remain. We hope the task force will be an impactful initiative for the advancement of Polar AI research, and encourage all interested stakeholders to take part.

We are looking forward to a productive workshop full of insightful talks and posters. The workshop will serve as a way for us to gather ideas and scientific perspectives to make the Task Force as effective as possible.
